liver: In mESC, regulatory elements are partitioned. Active elements show strong p300 and H3K27ac co‑occurrence (~52%), while Polycomb repression (H3K27me3) rarely overlaps (<1%). And TSS overlap patterns confirm this: ~16% of promoters are active (H3K27ac), ~4% are repressed (H3K27me3), and almost none carry both marks. This clean separation reflects the highly dynamic and bistable promoter architecture characteristic of pluripotent cells. In contrast, liver regulatory elements operate within much broader chromatin domains. While p300 still strongly overlaps H3K27ac (~59%), indicating active liver‑specific enhancers, Polycomb repression (H3K27me3) spreads across large genomic regions (~15% of TSS). These broad domains overlap many active promoters, yielding ~15% TSS that appear “double‑marked”, not because of true bivalency but due to the width of H3K27me3 peaks in differentiated tissue. Thus, the ESC enhancer landscape is flexible and sharply defined, whereas the liver enhancer landscape is stable and domain‑wide, reflecting committed cell identity. Overall, ESC regulatory elements show cleanly separated active and repressed states, while liver exhibits broad repressive domains and stable enhancer activation, highlighting a strong restructuring of regulatory architecture during differentiation.
